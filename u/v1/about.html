Born & bred in Cape Town, I’m proud of my country and the city I live in. The opportunity to travel was presented to me at a young age and I’ve continued to explore our world whenever possible. Most recently, my travels took me to Thailand and Cambodia. Prior to that, Germany, where I participated in the 2010 CRAFT Bike Transalp mountain bike stage race and before that, Australia where I have relatives who live there.

There’s no better way to explore your surroundings than on a bicycle. So when I’m not working, I enjoy cycling, whether it’s on the road or in the mountains. Photography is also something I enjoy doing, along with capturing videos of my outings on my GoPro camera.

Whenever possible, I try to enter and take part in races, whether it be running or cycling. Most of my results are available online. Using Garmin Connect, I can keep track of my performance and general fitness. And Strava is also fun to use from a social aspect to compare yourself to other athletes.

Inspiration

I read the 3rd edition of Designing with Web Standards by Jeffrey Zeldman. If you’re involved in any aspect of web development and haven’t read his book yet — stop whatever it is you’re doing right now and start reading! I really wish I had read this book earlier in my career as it would’ve made my life much easier.

After that, Adaptive Web Design by Aaron Gustafson was a great insight in to the world of progressive enhancement for me. To this day, I work hard to apply what I’ve learnt from this book in the projects I’m involved with.

More recently, with the rapid increase in the number of devices that can access the web, I’ve read two great books which I can highly recommend. Firstly, Responsive Web Design by Ethan Marcotte and Mobile First by Luke Wroblewski.
